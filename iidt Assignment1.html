Krishna And his Friends

Once upon a time in the sacred land of Vrindavan, little Lord Krishna and his friends Balarama, Sudama, and Madhumangal were playing near the Yamuna river.

They loved their carefree days, filled with laughter and joy. One sunny morning, Krishna suggested, "Let's go explore the forests beyond the river. I've heard tales of mysterious creatures living there."

Excited by the prospect of adventure, the boys eagerly crossed the river and ventured into the dense woods. They encountered playful monkeys, colorful birds, and exotic flowers along the way.

As they delved deeper into the forest, Krishna's mischievous nature sparked an idea. He whispered to his friends, "Let's play a prank on the village girls who come here to collect water. Hide behind the trees and mimic the sound of the flute."

With mischievous grins, the boys followed Krishna's lead. When the village girls arrived at the riverbank, they heard enchanting flute melodies echoing through the trees. Thinking it was Krishna, they giggled and called out his name.

But to their surprise, they found no one there. Confused, they searched high and low, but Krishna and his friends remained hidden, giggling with delight.

After a while, Krishna emerged from his hiding spot, his laughter contagious. The girls, initially annoyed, couldn't help but join in the merriment.

From that day on, Krishna and his friends continued their mischievous adventures, spreading joy and laughter wherever they went.
